Hello, World! I am currently a second-year student in the 
 <a href="http://www.hms.harvard.edu/md_phd/program/" target="_blank">Harvard/MIT MD-PhD program</a>. 
 I find it incredibly exciting that high-throughput sequencing technologies have essentially 
 enabled us to peer into the inner workings of cells, and I'm interested in developing and applying 
 computational methods to mine this data for new insights about disease biology.
 
I'm originally from Nashville, Tennessee, and I majored in Computer Science and Biology
 at Stanford ('18). For my honors thesis in the <a href="http://www.bhattlab.com" target="_blank">Bhatt Lab</a>, 
 I sequenced poop to study how certain bacterial species dominate the gut microbiome 
 after antibiotics. In college, I also had the opportunity to work (and 
 live!) at a <a href="http://thebridge.stanford.edu" target="_blank">24/7 peer counseling center</a>,
 run an <a href="http://intersect.stanford.edu" target="_blank">interdisciplinary research journal</a>, and volunteer 
 as a campus EMT. If you're interested in chatting about science, medicine, or where to get the best
 ramen in Boston, let's connect!
