Joyce Kang

Hello, World! I am an Harvard/MIT MD-PhD student working towards an MD through the HST program and a PhD in Bioinformatics & Integrative Genomics, under the direction of Soumya Raychaudhuri. I find it incredibly exciting that high-throughput sequencing technologies have enabled us to peer into the inner workings of cells, and I’m interested in developing and applying computational methods to mine this data for new insights about disease biology.

I’m originally from Nashville, TN, and majored in Computer Science and Biology at Stanford (class of ‘18). For my honors thesis in the Bhatt Lab, I sequenced poop to study genomic factors allowing certain bacteria to dominate the gut microbiome after antibiotic exposure. In college, I also had the opportunity to work (and live!) at a 24/7 peer counseling center, run an interdisciplinary research journal, and volunteer as a campus EMT. If you’re interested in chatting about science, medicine, or where to get the best ramen in Boston, let’s connect!
