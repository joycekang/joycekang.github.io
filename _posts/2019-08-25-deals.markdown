After a year of "adulting" in Boston, I've come across some cool deals that have definitely 
helped me have a little more fun on a student budget. Thought I'd share!

### <strong>ClassPass</strong>
Even though my dorm complex has a gym, I prefer working out at fitness studios 
because the ambience is nicer and the group setting boosts my motivation. I mainly like 
doing yoga classes, and more occasionally barre or indoor rowing. Becoming an actual 
member at one of these studios comes with unlimited classes, but it's too expensive for me
(<a href="https://www.yogaworks.com/location/back-bay/#membership" target="_blank">YogaWorks Back Bay</a> 
is $115/month and <a href="https://www.exhalespa.com/locations/boston/back-bay" target="_blank">exhale</a> 
is $180/month).

The compromise I've found is <a href="https://classpass.com" target="_blank">ClassPass</a>, a membership 
program that lets you go to fitness classes at various studios in Boston. The way it works 
is you get a certain number of "credits" per month (the lowest option is $49 per month for 
27 credits, which can book 3-5 classes). Although that may seem a little steep, it's cheaper 
than taking classes *a la carte* at the studio (usually ~$25 per class). They also have a sweet referral 
deal where you get $40 off your next month if you refer a friend to ClassPass. I've basically 
been roping in my med school friends one by one since March and have been paying **$9/month(!)**
for fitness classes this way. It's not ultimately sustainable but has been a pretty sweet deal so far!

Another option besides ClassPass is going to the weekly $10 Hip Hop Yoga "Community" class at 
<a href="https://www.yogaworks.com/location/back-bay/#membership" target="_blank">
YogaWorks Back Bay</a> on Saturday evenings. Some classmates and I would often go together 
then get dinner afterwards, which was a great way to escape the med school bubble!

### <strong>Boston Calling</strong>
I'm a huge fan of music festivals, and the biggest one that happens every year in Boston is
<a href="https://bostoncalling.com" target="_blank">Boston Calling</a>. 
This year, the lineup included Twenty One Pilots, CHVRCHES, Tame Impala
ODEZA, Hozier, Travis Scott, and Logic! General admission passes for the three-day festival
were around $250, but I was able to get in for free (and even get paid!) through their 
<a href="https://bostoncalling.com/staff/" target="_blank">Boston Calling 
Staffing program</a>. You work two 6-hour shifts (i.e. putting wristbands on people) and in 
exchange are paid $12/hr and free admission. I almost couldn't believe that I was getting
paid to see some of my favorite artists perform live! Some tips: only sign up if you'll be pretty free
that weekend, and sign up with a friend if possible so you have a buddy to work with!
